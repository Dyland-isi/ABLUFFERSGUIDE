RICO ACE: NATURAL TALENT IN BUCKETS

MUSIC May 2026

rico ace's music sounds like kawhi leonard's buzzer beater

on july 31, 2024, stay peckish released a music video that would change the trajectory of british rap as we know it. it featured two lads from liverpool, both in all black, rapping their hearts out over a beat with an addictive synth line. as well as serving as the opening salvo to a chapter in british rap that would ultimately see these two lads ascend to the top of the genre, it also features one of my favourite verses in recent memory, courtesy of one rico ace.

there's a debate raging at the moment on whether we should treat art as a sport. i think it depends on what form we're talking. i struggle to see how you could treat renaissance-style oil painting as a sport, but that's by the by. rap, for me, rap has always been the closest to a sport. you can train, and develop a style that suits your abilities, but crucially, natural talent is the biggest component. rico ace has natural talent in buckets. that verse exemplifies that better than i could ever articulate. it's funny, punchy, and delivered with such bravado and confidence that it genuinely took me aback the first time i heard it. this lad knows he has it, and is happy to let that talent fly. you have to back it. it seems others agreed.

you lot know the rest. collaborations with sinn6r followed, and, alongside longtime collaborator and mate esdeekid, rico has ascended to the top of the genre. culturally, he's at the forefront of a movement that rocked the genre. his verses tick every box you'd want from intense, dark, trap-adjacent rap music. intense delivery. narrative. wordplay. laugh-out loud funny one-liners. iconic bars. the kate moss bar on phantom hasn't left my brain since it entered. locked in.

on monday, rico ace touches back down in london. o2 kentish town. a big house. lad will no doubt tear it down. blackjack was strong. canny wait to see what he does next. one of our own.
