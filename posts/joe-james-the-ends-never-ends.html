JOE JAMES: THE ENDS, NEVER ENDS REVIEWED

MUSIC Jul 2026

i want to listen to joe james and drink a hugo spritz

i promise one day i'll stop harping on about the importance of environment when we listen to music. not today, though. few months ago, i'm walking through the park on the way to work. weak winter sunlight piercing through the gaps in the branches. hard to see. no warmth. but the skies were blue, for once. i skip to the next track. basildon. if there's such a thing as divine intervention, then the powers that be chose joe james to open spring. nice one. spring turned to summer, and basildon turned to same place. with summer peaking, joe james has released his debut album, the ends, never ends. sit tight.

it's been a long road for joe james. he's been making music for a decade plus at this point, and the quality has only continued to improve. back catalogue is aging like mediterranean architecture. sun-kissed. weathered. beautiful. his lyricism is rooted in grime, yet sonically, joe often finds himself implementing elements of jazz, blues, spoken word, and house. put those sounds together and baby, you've got a stew going. joe's previous projects are littered with rich soundscapes, witty wordplay, and his signature mellow delivery. bares mentioning too that the lad recently featured in stone island's community as a form of research campaign, alongside the likes of paolo maldini, liam gallagher, and spike lee. some company.

i've been sat with the ends, never ends for 11 days and, truthfully, i can't sit here and tell you i have a favourite song yet. too soon. take a touch. no need to rush in yet, for this is an album that requires some thought. joe james came to play. boasting contributions from dave, loyle carner, and lancey foux, the project is deeply touching, as joe reflects on his upbringing, troubled relationship with his mam, and formative days spent between london and essex. emotionally, it's a taxing listen in places. the headaches of old weigh heavy on joe throughout the project, yet add up to form one of the most impressive debut albums i've heard in a long time. a fantastic album, front to back. as summer starts to give way to autumn, i'll be keeping joe james on hand. what an artist.

nice one.
