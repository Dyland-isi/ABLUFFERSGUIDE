CEEBO'S MUSIC SOUNDS LIKE OUR TROUBLED YOUTH

MUSIC Jun 2026

british politics is in disarray again. nice one. here we go again on our own, going down the only road we've ever known. for my generation, that unfortunately rings true. we marked a decade since the brexit referendum this week, and since then, i've not known a day of political peace. from brexit, to covid, to the cost of living crisis, to today, the youth in this country have been passed from pillar to post. it's not on. we know that. it's reflected in the protests. in the age of our youngest and brightest politicians. and, in our art. enter ceebo.

for the record, ceebo makes bangers. music you can play at a party. room bouncing. arms waving. i've seen it first hand. i've been lucky enough to ceebo perform once or twice. i had a line is a fantastic song. blair babies though. lads. in moments of political instability, i find myself returning to this album. i listen to the opening monologue. nail on the head. hopelessness hangs heavy over our generation. the notion of being able to buy a house grows more distant with every economic forecast. we're veering into a dangerous place. apathy, bigotry, misogyny. our society's worst impulses are growing more pronounced. all these realisations swirl like mist around that monologue.

the rest of blair babies, while frequently touching on the darkness dwelling in every facet of british life, offers a beautiful respite. the music is fantastic. pulling from gospel, drill, and soul, the project is like a patronus charm. it acknowledges our situation, while shining the light that keeps us ticking over. buzzball summer. jook. luck of the chosen. ashawo interlude. these tracks see me daily. essential listening.

i know not where the path we're on takes us. will andy burnham steer us clear of reform? are we three years away from the most damaging premiership since thatcher? i have no idea. all i'm sure of is that ceebo will have something to say. when he talks, we listen. ceebo's a star.

class act.
