WE DO IT ALL FOR THE VERSUS CAPTION

ESSAY Jul 2026

art is all in the feeling. in whichever medium you hold closest. you can believe that an album is objectively one of the greatest of all time, but if you feel little when you listen to it, what was the point? the same goes for film, literature, sport, the lot. there have been better players than ronaldinho, but no one made me fall in love with football like he did. that's feeling. early days, when i was soaking up every article, blog, and archive page i could find, i was struck by their lack of emotion. you're telling me you love this artist, but it's not hitting. just words. then came versus.

of course, it had to be a 'woke up' post that got me on side. i'd seen versus before, but had never taken them in. silly me. i distinctly remember the turning point being their hugo rodallega post. naturally. the quintessential streets won't forget baller. i was locked in from that point onwards. versus has grown from strength to strength, working with some of the biggest brands, athletes, and artists in the uk and beyond, as well as breaking through into the mainstream cultural consciousness. their coverage of ebere eze formed the context for his arsenal reveal video, as well as the memes that followed arsenal's exit from the champions league, much to the chagrin of gooners everywhere. their feature with zohran mamdani felt like an inevitable cross over, and yet, that did nothing to diminish the joyous surprise it was. and most importantly, they produce some of the most essential opinion pieces in sports journalism. their words on sexual violence in football, the politics surrounding this year's world cup, and the treatment of women's football by some corners of the sporting world are vital. seek them out.

at its core, what keeps me coming back to versus is the feeling. they, unlike any others, exemplify the simple beauty of a well-chosen song, a handful of carefully chosen pics, and the written word. their posts aren't shakespeare in form, but they hit just as hard as any one of his monologues. they capture the heart and soul of the beautiful game. top drawer stuff. never stop.

nice one.
