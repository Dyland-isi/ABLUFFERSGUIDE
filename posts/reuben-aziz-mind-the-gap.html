REUBEN AZIZ HAS SAVED MY WEEK

MUSIC May 2026

been a rough one this week. taxing. some of you lot know this already, but day-to-day, i'm a journalist down in southampton. my job doesn't normally get to me, but it's been particularly tough going this week. i shan't go into it. have a read yourself if you fancy. as friday was drawing in, i really needed something to get me through. i had plans to go back to wales. long train journey ahead. as i stepped on, i gave reuben aziz's new project, mind the gap, a spin. it brought me back.

and how fitting that it's a southampton-born artist who brings me out of my funk. the universe just gets it sometimes. reuben aziz is having a moment. co-signs aren't the world, but if they were, reuben would be on top of it. stormzy. 4batz. the uk scene writ large. the people have fallen in love with his sound, and it's easy to see why. shotgun was a moment, and served as a snapshot as to why so many people are gravitating to his sound. melodic. melancholic. soothing. it's a winner.

mind the gap had been on my mind for a minute. the rollout fascinated me, and while i wasn't able to make it to any of the events in the lead-up to the project's release, my eyes and ears were open. it was exactly what i needed on friday evening. it's early days, but my favourite song so far has to be ego death. good tune. the decision to get travy to hop on was a stroke of genius. reuben's chipmunked vocals paired with skiifuego was always going to be a winning combination, but it's travy who shocks me here. his verse cuts through. the sharpness balancing out the sweet. i loved it. i'm still taking in the project, and i'm certain i'll have a new favourite song soon enough. but for now, reuben aziz, thank you for turning my frown upside down.

nice one.
